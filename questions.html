01 Does October line up with the wine harvest in Bordeaux or Rioja?

Yes — and Rioja especially. In Bordeaux the whites come off in September but the reds run from mid-September to mid-October, with Cabernet Sauvignon in the Médoc often still being picked in the first half of the month. On 6–7 Oct you have a good chance of catching sorting tables running and cellars fermenting. One catch: some châteaux restrict or reshape visits during vendanges — book ahead and ask for a "harvest-time" visit, which is the better show anyway.

Rioja runs later. Tempranillo comes off from late September through late October (Garnacha later still), so on 19–20 Oct around Haro the odds are good you'll see tractors queuing at the bodegas, smell fermenting must in the Barrio de la Estación, and get guaranteed autumn colour in the vines. Harvest is the single best time of year to be there.

Verdict: the dates are a feature, not a bug. Book winery visits early and mention you're happy to see harvest chaos.

Do this

  • Rue des Vignerons — book two Médoc/Saint-Émilion visits for 6–7 Oct and tick "harvest period is fine"
  • Muga / López de Heredia — reserve the Tue 19 Oct tours a week+ ahead (English slots are scarce)
  • Pack shoes you can ruin — harvest-time vineyard walks are mud
02 How much is the international one-way fee — and is the Hendaye trick worth it?

Two different fees get confused. Cross-border driving permission (taking a French car into Spain and back) is cheap: roughly €8/day at Sixt, ~€16 flat at Hertz, ~€30 capped at Avis, ~€55 at Enterprise. The expensive one is the international one-way drop fee for leaving a French car in Spain: typically €200–500+, occasionally quoted around €100 via brokers, occasionally far worse. It varies wildly by company, car class and month — the only real answer comes from quoting both scenarios when you book (Sixt, traveller reports).

The France-return alternative. From Rioja on the final day: Haro → Hendaye is about 1 h 50 (vs ~1 h 15 to Bilbao). Drop the car at Hendaye — the last French town, literally on the border, with rental desks near the international station — then the Euskotren "Topo" train crosses to San Sebastián every 30 minutes (~35 min, ~€3), and ALSA buses run San Sebastián → Bilbao every 30–60 minutes (~1 h 20, €7–18). Total extra time versus driving straight to Bilbao: about 2.5–3.5 hours, plus ~€25 of fuel/tolls and ~€15 pp of tickets.

Which town? Hendaye if the maths says go — it's the shortest possible detour and the Topo connection is painless. The alternative is Biarritz Airport (more agencies competing, so often better quotes, and direct FlixBus/ALSA coaches run Bayonne → Bilbao in ~2 h) — but it's 40 min further north, so only if its quote wins.

Verdict: worth it if the one-way fee quote exceeds ~€150–200; not worth an afternoon of your holiday to save €80. Quote both when booking the car and let the number decide. If you return in France, do it the morning of the 21st and treat the Topo + bus as part of the trip.

Do this

  • Get two quotes for identical dates/car: La Rochelle → Bilbao drop vs La Rochelle → Hendaye (or Biarritz Airport) drop — the difference is your answer
  • Euskotren — Topo timetable Hendaye → San Sebastián (every ~30 min, no booking needed)
  • ALSA — San Sebastián → Bilbao coaches; book the day before, €7–18
  • Whichever way, photograph the car at return — standard end-of-trip hygiene
03 What has October weather been like here the last few years?

Broad strokes from the national weather agencies' records:

October 2023 was exceptional — Spain logged its warmest October on record (AEMET called it a "veroño", summer-autumn) and France ran roughly 2 °C above normal; the Basque coast had genuinely summery spells with a swimmable sea, before Atlantic storms arrived late in the month.

October 2024 was mild but wetter, with regular Atlantic fronts sweeping the Bay of Biscay. Spain's month is remembered for the catastrophic late-October floods — those hit the Valencia region in the east, not this coast, but they're the reminder that October can turn violent fast.

October 2025 sat in another record-warm Spanish year: mild overall on the north coast with the usual mixed Atlantic pattern.

The climatology for Biarritz–San Sebastián in October: highs around 18–21 °C, lows 11–14 °C, ten-ish rain days, ocean still 17–19 °C. Inland Rioja runs a few degrees cooler at night and much drier.

Verdict: recent Octobers have leaned warm — expect T-shirt afternoons, wet spells, and one proper storm somewhere in the three weeks. Annette's packing rule (everything from summer kit to gumboots and a raincoat) is exactly right.

Do this

  • Packing list per person: raincoat, gumboots, one warm layer, swimmers, sunscreen — all of it earns its place
  • Check AEMET (Spain) and Météo-France ten days out and shuffle beach days accordingly
  • Hold the Guggenheim and the Balenciaga Museum as the designated storm-day cards
04 Any festivals or community events on the route while we're there?

The one to chase: the WSL surf comp. The world tour's France stop (Hossegor/Capbreton area) traditionally lands in an early-to-mid October window — if 2027's dates touch Fri 8 – Sat 9 Oct, the whole town becomes the event: heats off the beach, music, brand pop-ups. Watch the WSL schedule when the season is announced and book Hossegor beds the same day.

The near-miss: Fête du Piment, Espelette. The pepper festival — blessing of the peppers, bandas, 20,000 visitors — runs the last weekend of October (in 2027 that's the 23rd–24th, the day after the flight home; festival info). The village and its producers are open year-round though, and the cheese-hills detour already passes the door.

Possible bonus: Kilometroak. Gipuzkoa's roving Basque-language school festival takes over a different town one Sunday each October — street food, music, very local. If the 2027 edition lands on Sun 17 Oct anywhere near the coast road, detour to it. Date and village are announced closer to the year.

Just missed, for honesty: San Sebastián Film Festival and Logroño's San Mateo harvest festival both wrap in late September, Bayonne's big fêtes are July, and sagardotegi season proper opens in January (hence the "confirm October hours" note on the cider house).

Verdict: October is deliberately shoulder-season here — that's why it's cheap and calm. The surf comp is the one date worth engineering around.

Do this

  • WSL schedule — set a reminder for when the 2027 Championship Tour calendar drops; if France touches 8–9 Oct, book Hossegor that day
  • Google "Kilometroak 2027" in the new year — if the host town is near the coast road on Sun 17 Oct, fold it in
  • Espelette is already on the cheese detour — the AOP pepper shops don't need the festival
05 Is a Bilbao house-base worth it — and which barrio is affordable but central?

As the base for the whole Spanish leg: no. It fights the plan's best idea — one direction, a night in each small town, waking up in Mundaka instead of commuting to it. San Sebastián is 1 h+ each way and Rioja 1 h 15, and the big lunches don't mix with a drive home.

As a base for the final stretch: genuinely good. Everything from Sun 17 to Thu 21 orbits Bilbao anyway: Bermeo/Gaztelugatxe ~45 min, Plentzia 30 min (it's the end of the metro), Getxo 20 min, the airport 20 min. Renting one flat for those 4–5 nights means one unpack, a kitchen, and washing machines before the flight home. Keep the Ezcaray night on the 20th though — nobody should drive 1 h 15 after the Echaurren lunch.

Where: whole houses barely exist in central Bilbao — it's an apartment city (houses live out in Getxo/Plentzia on metro line 1, also a lovely option). For flats, the affordability-to-centrality winners are Santutxu (very affordable, proper local barrio, ~10 min by metro) and Bilbao La Vieja / San Francisco (the cheapest genuinely central option, arty and gentrifying, a bit scruffy late at night). Casco Viejo costs more but puts the pintxos outside your door; Indautxu/Abando is the most central and priciest. October ballpark for a 2–3 bedroom flat: roughly €110–190/night on the usual platforms.

Verdict: hybrid it — small-town beds along the coast as planned, then a Santutxu or Casco Viejo flat for the last 4 nights. Check the parking situation before booking; central Bilbao garages run €15–25/day.

Do this

  • Search a 2–3 bed flat for Sun 17 → Fri 22 Oct with the "free cancellation" and "parking" filters on — book refundable now, decide later
  • Compare one Getxo/Plentzia house against the city flats — beach suburb, metro line 1 straight in, parking solved
  • Map any candidate against a metro stop — Bilbao's metro is the whole trick to staying cheap and central at once
